Why Trinetra?

Our planet's oceans are facing unprecedented threats. Rising sea surface temperatures disrupt marine ecosystems, while the pervasive spread of microplastics and coastal pollution endangers life both in and out of the water. Understanding these complex issues requires a global perspective, yet traditional observation methods are often costly and slow.

Trinetra is our answer. By leveraging the power of compact, low-cost CubeSat technology, we aim to democratize access to critical ocean data. Our mission is to provide a continuous, high-resolution view of our oceans, empowering scientists, policymakers, and communities to take informed action.

This isn't just about collecting data; it's about creating a new paradigm for planetary stewardship, one where technology serves as a vigilant eye from orbit, watching over the blue heart of our world.

Inside Trinetra

A compact powerhouse of observation technology.

Structure

1U CubeSat Standard, Aluminum 7075-T6 chassis for maximum durability and minimal weight.

Power System

Body-mounted solar panels with a dedicated EPS managing a high-capacity Li-ion battery pack.

Mass

Total launch mass 1.33KG, optimized for rideshare launch vehicle requirements.

Dimensions

Stowed: 10x10x11.25 cm. Fully compliant with standard CubeSat deployers.

Orbit Type

Low-Earth Orbit (LEO) at an altitude of 450 km for consistent lighting conditions.

Thermal System

Passive system using multi-layer insulation (MLI) and surface coatings to maintain operational temperatures.

Q3 2025

Simulation & Analysis

Q4 2025

Fabrication & Prototyping

Building the first engineering model and beginning component manufacturing.

Q1 2026

Integration & Testing

Assembling the flight model and conducting rigorous system-wide tests.

Q2 2026

Ground Station Link

Establishing and testing communication protocols with our ground segment.
